What are National Parks?

National parks are large, protected areas of land that are preserved for their natural beauty, unique geological features and ecological diversity. They serve as sanctuaries to protect and preserve wildlife and environments, while providing recreational opportunities for visitors.

These parks often feature natural landscapes such as mountains, forests, canyons, and waterfalls, while also offering educational programs to promote environmental awareness and conservation.

Why conservation matters?

Conservation is crucial for maintaining biodiversity, protecting ecosystems, and ensuring the sustainability of natural resources for future generations. It helps to preserve the delicate balance of nature, supports wildlife habitats, and mitigates the impacts of climate change.

By conserving our natural environment, we can ensure that future generations can enjoy the beauty and benefits of nature while also supporting the health of our planet.
